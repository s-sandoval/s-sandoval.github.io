**About me**
======
Hello, I'm Santiago Sandoval Ruiz. I am a Sociologist (BA) from the Pontifica Universidad Católica _(Pontifical Catholic University)_ of Chile, and I'm currently studying a MA in Sociology in the same university.

My research focuses on social theory, historical sociology and the philosophy of social sciences. Theretically, I study how the social sciences theorize and constitute their empirical objects, analyzing specifically the many ways how the social sciences intervene in the world they try to study. Empirically, I  focus on the importance of the historical in the analysis of the social sciences by using case-studies of past events in order to test theories, hypothesis and illuminate current phenomena. Methodologically, I use qualitative techniques, mixing case studies with content analysis and in-depth interviews. 

I currently work as a research assitant for the Fondecyt N°11240326: ["Space and Time in Social Theory: Towards a Sistematization and Integration of Both Concepts"](https://www.tes-project.org/). 

I was born in [São Paulo, Brazil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo) through a Chilean family. Currently, I live in (Santiago de Chile)[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Santiago] (My _real_ hometown!).

Outside of academia, I've been involved in politics, mainly leading the [Memory and Human Rights Collective of the Universidad Católica](https://www.instagram.com/memoria.uc/) in the year 2021 and being elected into the [Student Federation of the Universidad Católica](https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federaci%C3%B3n_de_Estudiantes_de_la_Universidad_Cat%C3%B3lica_de_Chile) in 2022. Besides politics, I profoundly enjoy music: in my youth I hand a (indie-rock band)[https://www.instagram.com/equiponacional] and I regularly play the bass, guitar and piano. I also enjoy reading fictional books, mainly Latin-american poetry and novels.
